FAQ

  • 1. What is the difference between a designer and landscape architect?
    As a broad generalization, Landscape Architects tend to have more expertise, understanding and training, in hardscapes and complex grading and drainage situations and tend to focus more on large commercial and municipal projects. Landscape Designers typically work on residential landscape projects.

  • 2. What services does Sublime Garden Design provide?
    We provide a range of services including: Garden design and Landscape Architecture, landscape project management, and container gardens.

  • 3. What type of work do you do?
    We are a landscape design firm and therefore our focus is on design and maximizing the opportunities of each site. We specialize in sustainable and ecologically responsible residential landscape design. We enjoy working with homeowners and contractors throughout the process from design to installation to create beautiful and sustainable spaces that feel like an extension of your home. We can work with you to design and renovate an existing landscape or to create one from a blank slate.

  • 4. Do you do free consultations?
    We do not offer free consultations. Due to our workload we are only able to schedule a limited amount of consultations, so these appointments are scheduled only after we have spoken on the phone and determined that we would be a good fit for your project.

  • 5. Do I need a landscape design?
    A good landscape design can transform a yard you dread into a space you love by increasing the ‘useable living area’ of your property. If you have great beds and hardscape but need to refresh the plant pallet, a planting plan design may be all you need.

  • 6. I have never worked with a landscape designer before. What should I look for in a designer?
    Choosing a landscape designer or Landscape Architect can be a difficult decision. Our best bit of advice is to choose a person or company that you feel you can identify with in terms of their style, process, and personality. Then talk to that person and make sure that he/she listens to you and understands your needs. A good design should be a merging of your landscape designer’s expertise and eye, and your needs, wants, and resources. Your landscape is an extension of your home and a reflection of your personal style, and you should enjoy it to the fullest.

  • 7. What time of the year can you replant a tree?
    The optimal time of year to transplant tress and shrubs is either in the spring or fall seasons,due to low moisture stress on plants during those times of year.However,trees can successfully be transplanted throughout the year,as long as mitigating factors are addressed.

  • 8. Can you work with what I already have?
    Whether you want to keep your favorite plants in your new design or reuse all those plants, we can work with you to create a design that best suits your needs. When possible, we can reuse existing plant material in the new design. Just tell us what you love (and don’t love) about your landscape, and we’ll work with you to decide the best way to reach your goals.

  • 9. How do I take care of the newly installed landscape?
    A newly installed landscape will require regular watering while it establishes over the first 2-3 years. Even after that you may need to occasionally supplement water during our driest periods in July and August.

  • 10. Do you only design residential gardens?
    No, I have designed gardens for schools, commercial buildings, roadside beautification, but the bulk of my work has been residential or private or public space gardens, which is why I call myself a garden designer, versus a landscape designer.

  • 11. How big will the plants be when they are planted?
    This will depend quite a bit on your budget. The larger the root ball, the more expensive the plant. Specimen and slow growing plants are general specified in larger sizes. Fast growing plants and ground cover plants are generally purchased in smaller sizes. Different plants are grown in different sizes, and not available all year round. Depending on the time of year your installation is taking place, substitutions made need to be made. We prefer to source the plants for you and provide them for the landscape contractor to plant. The project site, scope and budget determines the plants when used in conjunction with each client's preferences.

  • 12. How long does it take for the garden to grow in?
    Plants grow at different rates and typically it will take five years which differs from place to place. Trees can take much longer than that, while ground cover and plants can look full after two years. Site conditions will also play a role in how fast a garden will fill in.
